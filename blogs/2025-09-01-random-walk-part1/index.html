Welcome to the first post in our series on the power of random walks! We’re diving into how a simple concept in graph theory can solve several challenges in understanding complex networks. We’ll be exploring the core ideas from our recent paper, “Learning Long Range Dependencies on Graphs via Random Walks”, which introduces an exciting new architecture called NeuralWalker.

Why Random Walks?

Graphs are everywhere: molecules, social networks, road maps, protein interactions. To learn from them, we need ways to explore structure and dependencies between nodes.

  • Message-passing GNNs excel at capturing local neighborhoods.

  • Graph Transformers capture global interactions, but often flatten the structure.

  • Random walks offer a third perspective: a natural way to traverse and sample graphs, bridging local and global structure.
